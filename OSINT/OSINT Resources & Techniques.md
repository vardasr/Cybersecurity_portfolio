   **ADS-B Exchange**
   * Description and Purpose: ADSB Exchange is an open-source aircraft tracking platform that provides real-time and historical flight data. Unlike many other flight tacking services, it does not filter or restrict military or private aircraft data, making it a valuable tool for OSINT investigations. The platform aggregates Automatic Dependent Surveillance-Broadcast (ADS-B) signals from aircraft worldwide, allowing users to monitor air traffic patterns, track specific aircraft, and analyze aviation activity.
   * Utility and Limitations:
     * Utility: provides unrestricted access to a wide range of flight data, including military and private aircraft, which is often filtered by other tracking platforms. It also allows for real-time and historical tracking, making it an essential tool for OSINT analysts.
     * Limitations: Data accuracy is dependent on voluntary ADS-B broadcasts, meaning some aircraft (e.g., those using military-grade encryption or not equipped with ADS-B) may not be visible. Additionally, ethical considerations must be addressed when tracking sensitive flights, ensuring compliance with legal and privacy guidelines. 
* Summary of Searches: Using ADSB Exchange, I conducted searches on various flight routes and aircraft activity in different regions. I tracked a specific commercial flight's real-time location, monitored historical data to observe repeated flight paths, and identified aircraft operating in restricted airspace. Additionally, I explored military aircraft movements in areas of geopolitical interest, ensuring that no sensitive personal or operational data was included in the findings.

  Geopolitical Analysis: Tracking military aircraft movements to infer defense operations or geopolitical tensions
